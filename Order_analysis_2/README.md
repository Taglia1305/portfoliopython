# NumPy Order Analysis Project

## Project Overview

This project is a complete NumPy-based business analysis workflow built on a simulated dataset of 1,000 customer orders.

The objective is to move from raw simulated order data to meaningful business insights by calculating economic KPIs, analyzing profitability, identifying outliers, and creating a business-oriented order segmentation.

The project focuses not only on technical calculations, but also on the interpretation of data from a managerial perspective.

---

## Project Objectives

The main goals of the project are to:

- Generate a realistic simulated order dataset using NumPy
- Calculate economic KPIs such as revenue, cost, margin, and margin percentage
- Analyze the distribution of margins through descriptive statistics and visualizations
- Study relationships between variables using correlation analysis
- Detect highly profitable or anomalous orders using the Z-score
- Segment orders according to profitability, satisfaction, and operational risk
- Produce a final business-oriented report based on the segmentation results

---

## Dataset Description

The dataset contains 1,000 simulated orders.

Each order includes the following variables:

| Variable | Description |
|---|---|
| `quantita` | Quantity purchased in the order |
| `prezzo_unitario` | Unit selling price |
| `costo_unitario` | Unit cost |
| `giorni_consegna` | Delivery time in days |
| `soddisfazione_cliente` | Customer satisfaction score |

The dataset is generated through random distributions, with a fixed seed to ensure reproducibility.

---

## Analysis Workflow

The notebook is structured into the following sections:

1. **Dataset creation**
   - Generation of quantities, prices, costs, delivery days, and customer satisfaction.

2. **Economic KPI calculation**
   - Revenue, cost, margin, margin percentage, and unit margin are calculated for each order.

3. **Descriptive analysis**
   - Mean, median, minimum, maximum, and standard deviation are used to describe profitability.

4. **Graphical analysis**
   - Histograms and scatter plots are used to analyze margin distribution and the relationship between quantity and margin.

5. **Correlation analysis**
   - A correlation matrix is created to evaluate linear relationships between the main numerical variables.

6. **Outlier detection**
   - The Z-score is applied to margins in order to identify highly profitable or anomalous orders.

7. **Business segmentation**
   - Orders are classified into different segments based on profitability and customer satisfaction.

8. **Final report**
   - Segment-level KPIs are summarized to provide a business interpretation of the results.

---

## Key Business Metrics

The project calculates the following main KPIs:

| KPI | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Total revenue | Total economic value generated by all orders |
| Total cost | Total cost associated with all orders |
| Total margin | Difference between total revenue and total cost |
| Margin percentage | Percentage profitability of the orders |
| Average margin | Average profit generated per order |
| Unit margin | Profitability per single unit sold |
| Customer satisfaction | Qualitative indicator linked to delivery performance |

## Outlier Detection

The Z-score is used to identify orders with margins that are significantly different from the average.

The thresholds used are:

| Threshold | Interpretation |
|---:|---|
| Z-score > 2 | Orders with a margin much higher than average |
| Z-score > 3 | Extremely profitable orders |
| Z-score < -2 | Orders with a margin much lower than average |
| Z-score < -3 | Extremely critical orders |

In this dataset:

- Orders with Z-score > 2: **42**
- Orders with Z-score > 3: **10**
- Orders with Z-score < -2: **0**
- Orders with Z-score < -3: **0**

This means that the dataset contains several highly profitable orders, while no extremely negative margin outliers are detected.

## Business Segmentation

The final part of the project classifies orders into business segments.

| Segment | Logic |
|---|---|
| Highly profitable but risky | Extremely high margin but low customer satisfaction |
| Profitable but risky | High margin but low customer satisfaction |
| Top performer | High margin and high customer satisfaction |
| Critical | Low margin and low customer satisfaction |
| Standard | Orders that do not fall into the previous categories |

Segment distribution:

| Segment | Number of orders |
|---|---:|
| Highly profitable but risky | 3 |
| Profitable but risky | 6 |
| Top performer | 24 |
| Critical | 24 |
| Standard | 943 |

The segmentation helps identify not only the best-performing orders, but also profitable orders that may carry a customer relationship risk due to low satisfaction or long delivery times.
